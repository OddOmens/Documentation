# Breathing Techniques

Learn about the science-backed breathing patterns available in Breatheful.

## Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

**Best for:** Focus, stress management, performance

Box Breathing, also known as square breathing, is used by Navy SEALs, athletes, and high-performers to maintain calm under pressure.

**Pattern:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts

**Benefits:**
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- Improves focus and concentration
- Regulates the nervous system
- Enhances performance under pressure

**When to use:**
- Before important meetings or presentations
- During stressful situations
- When you need to focus
- As a daily centering practice

## 4-7-8 Breathing

**Best for:** Sleep, relaxation, anxiety relief

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, this technique is often called a "natural tranquilizer for the nervous system."

**Pattern:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 7 counts
- Exhale for 8 counts

**Benefits:**
- Promotes deep relaxation
- Helps you fall asleep faster
- Reduces anxiety
- Lowers blood pressure
- Calms racing thoughts

**When to use:**
- Before bedtime
- When feeling anxious
- During panic or stress
- When you need to calm down quickly

## Deep Breathing

**Best for:** General relaxation, beginners

Simple, calming breaths with longer exhales to activate the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest mode).

**Pattern:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6-8 counts

**Benefits:**
- Activates relaxation response
- Reduces heart rate
- Lowers blood pressure
- Easy for beginners
- Versatile for any situation

**When to use:**
- Anytime you need to relax
- As a beginner starting point
- During meditation
- Throughout the day for mindfulness

## Coherent Breathing (5-5)

**Best for:** Balance, heart health, emotional regulation

Breathing at a rate of 5 breaths per minute has been shown to maximize heart rate variability and promote balance in the autonomic nervous system.

**Pattern:**
- Inhale for 5 counts
- Exhale for 5 counts

**Benefits:**
- Improves heart rate variability
- Balances the nervous system
- Enhances emotional regulation
- Promotes overall well-being

**When to use:**
- Daily practice for overall health
- When seeking emotional balance
- As a meditation foundation
- For cardiovascular health

## Energizing Breath

**Best for:** Energy, alertness, morning practice

A more active breathing pattern to increase oxygen and energy levels.

**Pattern:**
- Quick inhale for 2 counts
- Hold for 2 counts
- Quick exhale for 2 counts

**Benefits:**
- Increases energy and alertness
- Improves oxygen circulation
- Wakes up the body and mind
- Enhances mental clarity

**When to use:**
- Morning wake-up routine
- Afternoon energy slump
- Before exercise or activity
- When feeling sluggish

## Custom Patterns

**Best for:** Personalized needs

Create your own breathing patterns tailored to your specific needs and preferences.

**How to create:**
1. Tap "Create Custom Pattern"
2. Set timing for inhale, hold, exhale, and hold
3. Name your pattern
4. Save and use anytime

**Ideas for custom patterns:**
- Longer exhales for deeper relaxation
- Shorter patterns for quick breaks
- Specific counts that feel natural to you
- Patterns recommended by your therapist or coach

## Choosing the Right Technique

::: tip Finding Your Pattern
There's no one-size-fits-all approach. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Your needs may also change throughout the day or based on your current state.
:::

**For stress and anxiety:** Try 4-7-8 or Box Breathing

**For sleep:** Use 4-7-8 Breathing before bed

**For focus:** Box Breathing or Coherent Breathing

**For energy:** Energizing Breath in the morning

**For general wellness:** Deep Breathing or Coherent Breathing

**For specific needs:** Create a Custom Pattern
